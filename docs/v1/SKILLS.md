# Skills and Progression

## Launch skills

- Mining
- Woodcutting
- Farming
- Combat
- Refining
- Crafting
- Enchanting

Fishing is added only when Fishing itself is introduced.

## Shared progression model

- Level cap: 100.
- Early levels progress quickly; later levels become long-term mastery.
- No daily XP caps.
- Exact XP curves and reward values are configuration/balance values.
- Major milestones unlock meaningful tools/utility; incremental levels may improve continuous stats.
- High skill plus high-level gear may produce roughly an order-of-magnitude practical throughput advantage over a beginner when fully progressed. This is a target feeling, not a fixed numeric guarantee.

## Ownership vs use

Skill requirements restrict **use**, never ownership or trading.

A player may buy, store, sell, or prepare a high-level item before meeting its requirement. Production requirements are independent from use requirements: a crafter may produce a Mining tool they cannot personally use.

## Skill bonuses vs skill XP

These are independent definitions.

An action/resource may benefit from a skill's Speed/Luck/tool/pouch rules without being allowed to train that skill.

Example:

- Sand may benefit from Mining bonuses and Mining Pouch routing while granting 0 Mining XP.
- Authorized Iron/Quartz/Glowstone nodes may benefit from Mining bonuses and grant Mining XP.
- Player-placed blocks do not become progression loops.

This rule prevents low-value or easily repeatable actions from becoming optimal leveling exploits.

## Mining

Profession: ground/mineral extraction using the appropriate Minecraft tool.

Potential bonuses:

- Mining Speed
- Mining Luck/yield
- pickaxe and shovel tool unlocks
- Mining Pouch I-III
- later multi-block/vein-style manual tool effects

Shoveling is not a separate skill.

## Woodcutting

Potential bonuses:

- Woodcutting Speed
- Woodcutting Luck/yield
- axe unlocks
- Wood Pouch I-III
- later connected-log/tree-felling manual effects

Only authorized renewable trees grant economic output/XP.

## Farming

Potential bonuses:

- Farming Speed
- Farming Luck/yield
- better farming tools
- Farming Pouch I-III
- later multi-crop manual harvesting

XP comes from legitimate completed production cycles, not planting spam or breaking immature crops.

## Combat

Controls PvE-oriented combat progression and relevant equipment requirements. Ranked PvP uses standardized temporary state and does not inherit permanent Combat power advantages.

## Refining

Progression focuses on throughput, processing speed, batch/job capacity, and recipe access. Refining should not randomly create free material.

Reversible compression/decompression grants no Refining XP.

## Crafting

Progression focuses on recipe access and production capability. Crafting requirements gate creation; profession skill requirements gate use.

## Enchanting

Enchanting is an expensive meta-progression skill.

Its primary effect is a **Skill XP Amplifier** applied to XP earned in every other skill. It never amplifies Enchanting itself.

Enchanting progression consumes real Minecraft XP through the designated progression mechanism. XP bottles are only a tradable way to acquire normal Minecraft XP after use; they are not a direct Enchanting ingredient.

Enchanting should be expensive enough that there is no universally correct timing. A specialist may level only the skills they need first; a wealthy completionist may invest heavily in Enchanting to accelerate future skill progression.
